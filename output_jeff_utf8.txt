Concept 1: Volatility-Gated Decycler Breakout (Trend Regime)

ΓÇó Unique Entry Logic: Instead of a standard zero-line crossover, this strategy demands momentum. You enter Long only when the Decycler Oscillator crosses over its upper +2 Standard Deviation band, but strictly gated by the Volatility Regime confirming expansion. If volatility is contracting (mean-reverting regime), the crossover is ignored13.
ΓÇó Best Exit Combination: Donchian Trailing Stop + ATR Profit Target. Because this trades volatility expansion, you ride the trend using a longer-term Donchian channel (e.g., 20-bar lowest low) and take partial profits at a multiple of the ATR (e.g., 3x ATR)45.
Concept 2: Fractal Efficiency Decycler Fade (Cycle Regime)
ΓÇó Regime Adaptation: Uses Kaufman's Efficiency Ratio (Fractal Efficiency), which measures the ratio of directional price change to absolute price noise6. A low Efficiency Ratio (e.g., < 0.30) mathematically defines a highly noisy, Mean-Reverting (Cycle) Regime6.
ΓÇó Unique Entry Logic: In this choppy regime, standard zero-crosses whip-saw. Instead, you trade the rejection of the extremes. You enter Short when the Decycler Oscillator pierces the upper +2 Standard Deviation band and then crosses back under it, signaling the cycle peak is exhausted and reverting to the mean.
ΓÇó Best Exit Combination: Cycle Peak/Valley + strict Time Exit. You exit dynamically when the measured Dominant Cycle Phase reaches the opposing extreme (e.g., using a LeadSine crossover)78. A Time Exit (e.g., 5 to 10 bars) acts as a hard fail-safe if the cycle suddenly flatlines into a trend49.
Concept 3: ADX-Filtered Decycler Pullback (Trend Resumption)
ΓÇó Regime Adaptation: Uses the Average Directional Index (ADX). An ADX rising above 25 confirms a persistent, established Trend Regime10....
